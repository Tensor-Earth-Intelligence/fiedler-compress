## What this measures

Answer **survival under compression**: for each item the model answers a question from a
document, at a range of compression levels; survival is the fraction of items still
answered correctly. The point of interest is how survival falls as more of the context is
removed, and whether pinning the answer-bearing content recovers it.

## Methodology

- **Corpus:** multi-passage SQuAD v1.1 (CC BY-SA), **N=150** items, seed
  20260620. Each item is **k=5** concatenated SQuAD paragraphs
  (one gold-answer-bearing + distractors; distractors that contain the gold span are
  rejected). Single SQuAD paragraphs are too short to exercise a real 1.5–5x sweep (they
  fall below the compressor's min-chunk floor and pass through unmodified), so passages are
  concatenated to give the method enough chunks to compress.
- **Compression sweep:** `target_ratio` in [0.33, 0.5, 0.6, 0.67, 0.75, 0.8] (fraction removed). Because
  removal is whole-chunk, the **achieved** compression can plateau below the nominal target;
  we therefore report **achieved** token compression and factor per condition, and adjacent
  nominal targets can collapse to one achieved level. `cpin80` =
  most-aggressive ratio with the gold answer phrase content-**pinned** (never removed).
- **Two deterministic evaluators** (no LLM judge), scored on the same outputs:
  **EM** = normalized substring/exact-match containment of a gold span (binary); **F1** =
  official SQuAD v1.1 token-level F1 (continuous). EM requires the full gold span verbatim,
  so it is a conservative lower bound that undercounts concise answers; F1 is the
  article/punctuation-robust canonical metric. Both are reported.
- **Mem-leak control:** every question is also asked with **no** document. Items answered
  correctly with no context test training memory, not retrieval; both raw and mem-leak-
  **filtered** numbers are shown.
- **Statistics:** percentile bootstrap **95% CI** (10,000 seeded resamples) on the
  per-item score array for each (model, condition, evaluator).
- **Provenance:** host `FiedlerMark`, fiedler-compress
  `0.1.1`, code fingerprint
  `f8f246712c12`, 3 reps/item, temp
  0.1.

## How to read this

- **Survival falls monotonically with compression** under the query-agnostic method, then
- **content-pinning (`cpin`) restores survival to ~baseline even at ~5x compression** —
  a training-free way to protect known-salient content.
- **EM vs F1 gap is model-dependent**: verbose models score EM >> F1 (the gold span is
  present but padded with extra tokens); concise models score EM ≈ F1.
